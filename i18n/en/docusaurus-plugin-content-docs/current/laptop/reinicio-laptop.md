# Laptop restart

After a power outage, the laptop can end up in an unstable state. Even if the docking station works, the laptop may not detect the connection properly until it is restarted.

Before you begin:
Take your time with the procedure. You do not need tools or technical knowledge.

## What you will do

Disconnect the laptop from the docking station, release residual power, and reconnect it safely.

## Step by step

1. Disconnect only the **USB-C cables that go from the docking station to the laptop**.

   ![USB-C cables from the docking station to the laptop](/img/cables.png)
   *USB-C cables from the docking station to the laptop.*

2. With the laptop disconnected from the docking station, press and hold the power button for **10 to 15 seconds**.

3. Wait a few seconds before reconnecting the cables. This helps the laptop finish releasing any residual power.

Avoid mistakes:
Do not disconnect cables that are not part of the connection between the docking station and the laptop.

4. Reconnect the USB-C cables from the docking station to the laptop. Make sure they are firmly seated and in the correct position.

5. Press the power button **once** to start the laptop.

## Final check

Confirm that the laptop is ready to work:

- The screen turns on normally
- The docking station detects the laptop again
- Connected peripherals work
- The network connection is available, if applicable

If the laptop still does not respond, repeat the process once and verify that the USB-C cables are connected correctly. If the problem persists, please contact IT.
