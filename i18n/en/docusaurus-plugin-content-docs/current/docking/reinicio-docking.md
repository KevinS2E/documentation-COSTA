# Docking station restart

After a power outage, the docking station may have residual power or an unstable connection. That can prevent monitors, USB ports, or the network from responding correctly.

Before you begin:
If something does not work, do not force the cables. This guide is meant to fix common issues after a power outage.

## What you will fix

Reset the docking station to recover monitors, USB, and network after a power outage.

## Step by step

1. Identify the power cable.
   Look for the docking station's **round power cable**. On many models, the connector has a **blue light** when it is connected. In some cases the light is white, and in a few models there is no light.

   ![Back of the docking station showing the power connector](/img/docking.png)
   *Back of the docking station showing the power connector (last one from left to right).*

2. Disconnect the docking station power cable and, if applicable, also unplug the power adapter from the wall outlet.

3. With the docking station unplugged, press and hold the power button for **15 seconds**. This helps release residual power and reset the device's internal state.

Avoid confusion:
Do not disconnect USB cables or video cables in this step. Only remove power from the docking station.

4. Reconnect the power cable and wait a few seconds for the docking station to finish starting up.

## Final check

Confirm that these items work again:

- Monitors
- USB
- Network

If any of them still do not respond, check that the docking station is connected correctly and repeat the procedure once. If the problem persists, contact IT.
